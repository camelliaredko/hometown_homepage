4-H Children's Garden

4-H Children's Garden is an outdoor garden complex inside Michigan State University. It first opened in 1993 and was the first garden in the United States developed to educate young children on a university campus. It draws in over 100,000 visitors per year and is nationally renowned for its technological innovations. The garden is a great place for children to learn about botany and science; it also offers interactive activities to help students develop their green thumb. Its mission is to promote understanding of plants and the roles they play in our lives, nurture the wonder, imagination, and curiosity of a child, and provide a spot of delight and enrichment in children of any age. Your kids can visit their themed gardens with names like Sunburst Entrance or Imagination Arbor and take their Butterfly Tours to study butterflies and make related crafts.

East Lansing

East Lansing is a city in the U.S. state of Michigan. Most of the city lies within Ingham County with a smaller portion extending north into Clinton County. At the 2020 Census the population was 47,741. Located directly east of the state capital of Lansing, East Lansing is well-known as the home of Michigan State University.
